# Steps for Writing Sample Collection

This document outlines the steps you should follow to complete the Writing Sample Collection task effectively.

## 1. Understand the Assignment
- Refer to the [README](./writingSampleCollection/README.md) for detailed instructions and requirements.
- Review the assessment criteria to understand how your work will be graded.

## 2. Plan Your Structure
- Use the [Outline Template](./writingSampleCollection/outline_template.md) to plan your Introduction and Literature Review.
- Identify the moves (steps) required for each section.

## 3. Gather Feedback
- Collect feedback from instructors, peers, and supervisors using the [Comments Template](./writingSampleCollection/comments_template.md).
- Document the context and key points of all feedback received.

## 4. Reflect on Your Writing
- Identify your writing challenges and strengths using the [Reflection Template](./writingSampleCollection/reflection_template.md).
- If using AI tools, document how they assist your writing and improvements needed.

## 5. Start Drafting
- Use the [First Draft Template](./writingSampleCollection/firstDraft_template.md) to create drafts for the Introduction and Literature Review.
- Follow the "Moves" structure provided in the templates.

## 6. Submit Writing Samples
- Select and refine 2-3 paragraphs using the [Writing Samples Template](./writingSampleCollection/writingSamples_template.md).
- Focus on clarity, coherence, and adherence to the "Moves".

## 7. Reference Previous Work
- Include relevant sections of previous academic writing using the [Previous Writing Template](./writingSampleCollection/previousWriting_template.md).
- Highlight why this work is relevant and how it informs your current task.

## 8. Final Review
- Ensure all templates are completed and link back to the main requirements in the README.
- Proofread all sections for language accuracy and coherence.

## 9. Submit Your Task
- Follow the course submission guidelines to upload your completed task for review.
